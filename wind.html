What happens to Wind Speed the further you move from the equator?

Figure 4 illustrates wind speed values when you move either north or south of the equator

Locate the center of the figure, at 0, that is the equator.

Generally, wind speed was between 0 and 20 miles per hour across the globe.

Move north (positive numbers) and there is not much variation between the wind speed values than if you had moved south of the equator (negative values).

But there are a number of observations above 20 piles per hour that are clustered betwwen 40 degree and 80 degree latitude.

The windiest places on earth on January 5 2017 were above 40 degrees latitude.
